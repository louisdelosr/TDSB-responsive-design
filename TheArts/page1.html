inspire ARTS

Elementary teachers gathered with Instructional Leaders in the Arts for full day experiential learningsessions, designed for teachers with little or no experience in the arts. These sessions provided primary, junior and intermediate teachers with scaffolded learning opportunities in dance, drama, visual arts and arts integration. A focus on implementing current assessment practices and lesson/unit design allowed teachers to explore how to use the creative and critical analysis processes as a foundation for building strong arts programs.

“I was able to apply the things I learnedimmediately into my classes and they were well received by the students. In fact, my arts programs have improved significantly with the workshop activities and handouts (including lesson plans and the very useful dance music compilation). Thank you for putting programs together that help us strive for excellence in the Arts in practical and attainable ways.”

- Claudine Tosso, Grade 7/8 Teacher,Alexander Stirling P.S.

innovateARTS

Arts teachers from more than 20secondary schools gathered for a fullday to begin an important dialogue. Dance, Drama, Media Arts and Visual Arts teachers considered whatinnovation in arts education looks like today. Together they investigated some of what is happening in thecontemporary art world and imagined ways they will continue to bring this thinking into our arts classrooms and to the lives of the students we teach.This launched ongoing dialogue between teachers and inspired many teachers to participate in Dare2Create with their students.

Tuesdays at ArtReach: An Art Studio Drop-in for Teachers Elementary (Grades K-8)

Over a series of 9 months elementary teachers joined Visual Arts Instructional Leaders once a month to investigate artmaking processes such as painting, drawing, printmaking,sculpture and mixed media. Teachers learned how to order,manage and use a range of open ended art materials that are essential in all art programs. The approaches made art-making accessible for all and strategies for looking at, talking about and assessing art were integrated through each session.

“I really did find out that I can create engaging art that communicates amessage or feeling. Now I have the confidence to teach these approaches to my students - tomorrow!”

- Donna Signori, Teacher Participant

Professional Learning for Teachers

A variety of workshops were offered to enhance and deepen the knowledge and understanding of teaching and learning in the arts. These hands-on, experientialprofessional learning opportunities were facilitated by professional artists, internationally recognized educators and Instructional Leaders, with a focus on current arts pedagogies and innovations. On November 15th, 250 Artsteachers attended a professional learning session, “Creative Impulses, Critical Thinking and Cultural Connections” at the AGO. Teachers were invited to examine the creativity and critical theory that underlies their own arts teaching practice and make connections to the innovative work of contemporary interdisciplinary artists. The session culminated with critical analysis activities during a tour of the David Bowie exhibition.

Dance & Drama Drop-in Series Elementary
(Grades K-8)

Once a month, teachers attended professional learning sessions led by Instructional Leaders and guestartists to investigate dance anddrama techniques in a safe and engaging setting. This experiential module supported teachers with techniques and resources tostructure dance and drama lessons, approaches to understandingcreative and critical processes, and a variety of assessment practices. Topics included: Starting your program, Assessment, Elements of Dance, Critical literacy and Sharing Best Practices.

Exploring the Arts in K-1

The Arts, Early Years, and Music Departments collaborated to create this module designed to support 50 kindergarten, Grade 1 and Early Childhood Educators. Teachers incorporated the languages and methodologies of the arts to explore ways of developing thinking, and expressing emotions in young children. Teachers explored effective ways to assess, and develop creative and critical thinking and problem solving in their classrooms. There was a focus on building safe and positive learning arts communities and teaching through a lens of social responsibility.

Imagining My Sustainable City
Appreciating Place and Envisioning a Future Toronto.

A Partnership with No. 9 Grade 7/8 students learned about sustainable design and civic responsibility through a partnership project with No.9. Architects-educators teamed with teachers to engage student sin the design and construction of architectural models for a specific site in their community. Discussions of civic engagement, governance and living a sustainable lifestyle informed the students’ designs. The project infused the real-world, interdisciplinary aspects of thearchitectural profession with the Grade 7-8 curriculum. STEM and the Arts were central to the learning process.

Arts in Education Co-op Program: Across Experiences

Across Experiences is a unique arts-based co-op program facilitated by the Arts Department in collaboration with the Aboriginal Education Centre. Secondary students with an interest in social justice and a passion for the Arts devised original works in dance, drama, film based on themes of intersecting identities, relationships, and human rights. The final works, called Intersections, toured grade 7/8 classrooms in May and June reaching approximately 2,000 students with powerful, artistic messages about inclusion and respect for diversity. The program culminated with a student matinee and gala evening performance at Young People’s Theatre.

ArtsJunktion

ArtsJunktion solicits, collects, and stores materials from hundreds of donor companies and waste recycling organizations. Approximately 4500 teachers visited the center this year, using the assorted materials for a range of creative projects in their classrooms.

Dare2Create Arts Festival
Co-presented by the TDSB and Toronto Arts Council

Dare2Create 2014 is the first annual system-wide celebration of dance, drama, media arts and visual arts in the TDSB. The festival took place between May 12 and June 10 in schools, communities, galleries, and performing arts venues around the city of Toronto. Students, teachers,and artists came together as a community to create, celebrate, and learn together. Approximately 5,000 students and their teachers, and 120 artists participated in the festival.

Dare2Create is a Celebration of Student Voice

Leading up to, and over the duration of the festival, students and teachers partnered with artists to explore their personal connections to the people, places, histories, and happenings of their city. Students of all ages were encouraged to say something powerful about through dance, drama, film, media arts, photography, spoken word, or visual arts.

Dare2Create is a Celebration of our City

The range of creative projects undertaken by students, with the support of artists and teachers, provided a rich range of youth perspectives on life in the city. Public showings of student work allowed to understand our city, anew, through the eyes of our children, from Kindergarten to Grade 12.

Dare2Create is a Celebration of Arts and Creativity for All

This celebration of the arts opened up opportunities for more artist engagement in school communities. The project aimed to inspirestudents, their families, and teachers inspired to embrace arts andculture as an essential component of a well-rounded education and fulfilling life. The public celebration of student work also acknowledged the important role that artists and teachers play in nurturing creativity and artistic expression.

“At Dare2Create, I learned that you can be a poet just like Mustafa, and it doesn’t matter how young or old you are to have your work admired.”

- Aliyah, Gr. 7 student, Ionview PS

Cultural Hotspots

On May 2 the TDSB Arts Department and the City of Toronto’s Cultural Hotspot presented the Careers For Creatives, a conference for 60 secondary students about careers in the arts. The conference took place at Cedar Ridge Creative Centre. Students were informed of the multiple paths open to them through the arts to careers and education in any field and engaged in a variety of hands-on art makingactivities and conversations with working artists andcreative entrepreneurs.Toronto-based artist,Che Kothari, provided aninspiring key-note address and every student left with a resource guide featuring educational paths, possible careers, workshop information and volunteer opportunities.
